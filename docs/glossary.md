# Glossary

## Alpha

A signal or rule that attempts to forecast relative return. In this repository, alphas are discussed by family, not by private formula.

## Backtest

A historical simulation of how a strategy might have behaved under specified assumptions.

## CAGR

Compound annual growth rate. A historical annualized return estimate.

## Cost Sensitivity

A test that increases assumed trading costs to see whether a result remains plausible.

## Drawdown

The decline from a prior portfolio peak to a later trough.

## Execution Realism

The practice of checking whether a backtest accounts for trading delays, costs, slippage, fills, and turnover.

## False Discovery

A result that looks strong in testing but is likely caused by chance, overfitting, or excessive search.

## Lag Sensitivity

A test that delays signals or trades to see whether performance depends on unrealistic timing.

## Live Validation

Monitoring a model after development using new observations. Public logs should be delayed and aggregate.

## Out Of Sample

A test period not used to design or fit the strategy.

## Regime

A market state, such as higher-volatility or lower-volatility conditions, used to contextualize model behavior.

## Robustness

The degree to which a result remains plausible across alternative assumptions, samples, and stress tests.

## Sharpe Ratio

A risk-adjusted return measure: average excess return divided by volatility, usually annualized. A high historical Sharpe is not a performance guarantee.

## Sleeve

A component strategy or signal family within a larger portfolio.

## Turnover

How much of a portfolio changes during rebalancing. Higher turnover usually increases implementation cost.
